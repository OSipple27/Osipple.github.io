Career & Future Career Goals

While serving in the USAF, my primary job was in Security Forces. Still, I spent most of my career as a dispatcher or an executive assistant. A fun fact about me is that; back in 2014, I served a year-long tour of duty at Osan Air Base. I was selected out of five candidates (who all outranked me at the time) to be an Executive Assistant to my Commander. The 51st Security Forces Squadron is the largest Security Forces unit in the Pacific Air Force and was established in 1950. I was the first black male executive assistant in the entire history of that squadron's existence. Once I separated in 2016, I focused on school and worked in a few different positions. What common trend I started noticing in all of my jobs is that I was always the go-to tech guy. It took me some time, but I realized I had a tech niche and should build on that foundation. Currently, I'm completing my B.S. in Computer Science at South Western College in Kansas with only six classes remaining. Also, I've begun studying for my "Comp-tia Security +" certification. My goal for 2023 is to complete my degree and obtain my first certification simultaneously to jump into my tech career. Further down the road, I would like to complete my Master's in Public Administration and start a non-profit within the inner city that assists children in the inner city in obtaining those tech certifications that don't require a college

Are You A Groom About to Get Married?

As I stated previously, I became a Husband this year to my now wife, Asia. Initially, when we started planning, I thought what most men think: "I will just take the back seat," but I quickly learned that is the wrong attitude to have. Just think, this is your big day too! All your friends and family will witness you take one of the most significant steps in your life, so why not have a little input? Overall the process can be stressful, but hopefully, with guidance, you can change that.

Your Suit, Your Best Man's Suit, and Your Groomsmen Suits

When choosing your suit, remember that you can't outshine your Bride but everyone else...upstage them all! As you can see in my photo, I wore a white suit with gold and almond undertones. When choosing your suit, ask your wife about the colors in her dress. Knowing this information will ensure you compliment the Bride, especially when photos are being taken. Your suit should be something simple but not as simple as the best man or groomsman. Again, you want to stand out on your big day! For your Bestman and your Groomsmen, think about the wedding colors and try to select the earth tone or best accent color in your color wedding color scheme. For example, our colors were white, gold, sage, and burnt orange. Of course, the Bride & groom wore white, the bridesmaids wore sage, and the groomsmen wore burnt orange suits with sage bowties to compliment the bridesmaids.
